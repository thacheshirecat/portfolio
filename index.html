About Me

I was born in the city of Alexandria, Virginia to Julia and Paul Bunnell. My earliest years were spent in Alexandria and then in the city of Herdon. By the age of six, my mother and I had moved back to Alexandria into a town home that my grandparents had invested in back in the 70s. I attended private middle and elementary school as my mother was adament that a good education was one of the keys to success in life. I was always held to a high academic standard and was eventually accepted to a private high-school in Washington D.C. not far from where we were living. However, while the education was excellent I never quite fit into the culture of the school. I did take two computer science classes in highschool, both focusing on Java, however in the end I decided to pursue philosophy in college as at the age of 18 I really did not know what I wanted to do with my life.

Current Interests

I, like many others around my age that grew up in the U.S., grew fond of video games at a young age (purchased first console, SNES, at the age of 7). I enjoy being able to explore new worlds and take part in stories too fantastical for real life, as well as optimizing and theorycrafting. For many years I wanted to be a video game developer, however always felt that this was not something that people would take seriously, and also recognized the various sterotypes surrounding gamers with no coding/project experience wanting to be game devs. Over time, and after moving to Seattle, I interacted with more people who had the same dream, but who had followed it through to the end. These interactions, along with whatever wisdom I acquired as I grew older, finally convinced me that I needed to take a step, even if it wasn't directly towards the end of being a game dev. I may never become an actual game developer, however gaming, art, coding and creativity will always be a part of my life.
